Condensed Milk Bread!

Country originated in: Japan
Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients:
- 250g all purpose flour
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 3g yeast (active/instant)
- 3/4 cup warm milk (110F)
- 30g cubed unsalted butter(room temperature)
- Fillings (optional) Walnuts/cream cheese/cranberries/raisins

Directions:
1. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt together.
2. If you are using active dry yeast, make sure you let the yeast and sugar sit in the warm milk for 5-10minutes. For instant yeast, you can just mix them with the flour immediately.
3. Pour the yeast mixture into the dry ingredients. Add condensed milk. Mix until the mixture comes together
4. If you have a stand mixer, you can use it. Otherwise, you can hand knead it.
5. Transfer the dough into the work surface. Use a rubbing motion at first because it is too sticky to throw. It should take around 10-15minutes. Once it does not stick to your hand, add in butter. Make sure you mix the butter that is on the table as well. Continue kneading for 5-10 minutes. Once it does not stick to your hand, you can use the window pane test to see if it is well kneaded.
6. Put the dough into a well greased bowl. Cover with plastic cling wrap or towel and let it rise in a warm place for 2h or until double in size.
7. If you want to add filling, feel free to chop the walnuts.
8. Once it is risen, transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Punch down the air and divide into 8 equal pieces. Press down the dough and add in fillings you like. Shape each piece into a ball.
9. Place the rolls onto a baking pan with parchment paper. Cover and let it rise in a warm place for 1h
10. Preheat the oven at 350F.
11. You can brush the rolls with milk or egg wash(1 egg with 1tbsp cold water) if you like the golden colour.
12. Bake at 320F for 10 minutes then at 320F for 17minutes.
13. Once they are baked, let it cool to room temperature. Feel free to sprinkle with icing sugar.

Tips
1. If the environment is too cold, you can put the bowl or pan onto a heater. Or put the bowl or pan into the oven without turning it on. Place warm water next to the bowl. This helps create a warm environment for gluten to develop
2. When you are kneading, the dough is really sticky. Be patient and continue kneading. Use a scrapper to help. Donâ€™t flour the dough when you are kneading. Eventually, it will come together and your hands will be clean.
